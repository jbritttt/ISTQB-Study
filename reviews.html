What is the review process and what does it involve?

Introduction
Review range from informal to formal
Formality related to
Maturity of organisation and development process
Can involve only two people author and review
Normally involves peers of the author
The goal is to find defects or areas for improvement
Informal reviews are not documented.

Involves the structured examination of documentation
Carried out by people with a spread of expertise
Checklists are used to structure reviews
Can use automated tools.

What are some review process activities?

Planning
Review Initiation
Independent Review ISO/IEC 20246
Communication and analysis.
Fixing and reporting.

Manager
Moderator
Author
Scribe
Reviewers

What is the managers role in a review?

Gives appropriate level of organisational priority to reviews
Decides on the execution of reviews
Allocates Time in Project Schedule
Determines whether review objectives have been met
Organises training for participants

What is the moderator role in a review?

Leads the review of documents
Plans the review
Ensures smooth running of meetings
Does meeting follow up
Mediation

What is the author's role in a review?

The person with chief responsibility for the documents under review

What is the reviewers role in a review?

People from a range of backgrounds that, together, have the necessary expertise to carry out their role. Examine documents (preparation) and then meet to discuss, in a structured format, the quality of the documents.

What is the scribe's role in a review?

Documents all issues, problems and open points raised during the meetings.

What is the review leader's role in a review?

Takes overall responsibility for the review such as deciding who will be involved, and organizing when and where the review will take place

What happens during the planning phase of a review?

Author requests a review
Request goes to moderator
Moderator reviews documents to ensure they have reached entry criteria (standards)
Moderator marshals related documentation
Moderator and author determine how many pages should be reviewed in a session
Moderator sets time for kick off meeting and invites participants

What happens in the kick off phase of a review?

Initial review-team meeting
Participants are briefed on the objectives and scope of the review
Documents are provided to participants
Relationships between documents are explained
Roles are assigned
Checking rate (pages per hour) given
Pages to be checked
Questions answered

What happens in the preparation phase of a review?

Participants work individually on documents under review
They make use of
Related Documents
Checklists
Rules
Procedures
Checking rate participants should be clear about the number of pages to check per hour usually 5 10.
Reviewers log issues in preparation for the meeting

What are the three phases in a review meeting?

Logging
Discussion
Decision

What happens on the rework phase?

Based on defects detected
Author will rework the document to fix issues
Changes should be tracked so that the moderator can confirm fixes.

What happens on the Follow up phase?

Moderator ensures that all satisfactory actions have been taken
Moderator distributes reworked documents to reviewers and collects their feedback
In order to measure, control and optimise the process the moderator collects metrics at each step of the process
Defects Found
Time spent checking per page
Total review effort
